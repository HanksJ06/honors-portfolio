Gateway

This is where all my Honors Experiences will go.

WHAT

During my first IT internship, I worked on a project that involved creating a timer in JavaScript to record time spent on GitHub and send the data to a database. My primary role was designing and managing the database, while my partner focused on the timer function and Chrome extension. The project was challenging, as we had to learn new technologies quickly and collaborate effectively to meet deadlines. The culmination of our work was a presentation to our supervisors, explaining the project’s functionality and impact.

STRENGTHS/VALUES/IDENTITY:

  • Strengths: Problem-solving, perseverance, and teamwork played a huge role in navigating this experience. I had to debug issues in the database, ensure data integrity, and communicate with my partner to align our work.
  • Values: I value learning and growth, which helped me embrace the steep learning curve of new tools and frameworks.
  • Identity: As an IT student, I identified strongly with my role, which motivated me to push through challenges and deliver a functional product.

Visual Artifact:

SO WHAT:

This experience reinforced the importance of adaptability and collaboration. Initially, I struggled with database structuring, but through trial, error, and research, I found effective solutions. Presenting our work also boosted my confidence in articulating technical concepts.

NOW WHAT:

Moving forward, I will apply these lessons in my academic projects, co-ops, and future IT roles. Effective communication and adaptability will be essential in my upcoming work trip to Japan, where I’ll collaborate with diverse teams. Additionally, the perseverance I developed will help me tackle new challenges in game development and networking.
